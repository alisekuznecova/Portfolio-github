Typography

In a period of Modernism, the idea that “form must fallow functionality” was a decisive element not only in architecture or visual arts, but as well as in typography- since the aim of the written text is to communicate, to give information and the value of the text lies in it’s content and purpose, the emphasis when choosing the right typeface was put on simplicity and legibility. In order to better understand typography role in modernism, it is worth mentioning a quote by Beatrice Ward : "type well used is invisible as type."

Before widely used serif fonts were changed by sans serif - simple and without any unnecessary decorative elements. Classical examples of most used typefaces in the era of modernism would include Franklin Gothic, Monotype Grotesque, Futura and Helvetica Neue.
